# ADR-069: Experimental modular aerial fire blanket capability

- **Status**: proposed
- **Date**: 2026-07-21
- **Deciders**:
- **Tags**: aerial-deployment, firebreak, research

## Context

The platform proposes deploying guided ground robots with a modular fire-resistant membrane ahead of predicted ignition or fire spread to protect corridors/assets and buy time for containment. No 100-robot, 100-panel, approximately 15-acre tethered aerial membrane system has demonstrated safe flight, extraction, deployment, landing, anchoring, thermal performance, or wildfire effectiveness.

## Decision

Treat the aerial fire blanket as an experimental, progressively promoted capability—not an available production feature. Its allowed purposes are pre-ignition shielding, emergency firebreak support, ember/radiant-heat reduction, and temporary infrastructure/corridor protection within an approved ODD. It is prohibited over developed crown fire, people, occupied evacuation routes, uncontrolled airspace, or conditions outside certified wind/smoke/terrain/material envelopes. Begin with ground deployment, then low-altitude unmanned tests, subscale aircraft extraction, partial-scale transport-aircraft tests, and only then a full configuration. Each stage has independent go/no-go evidence and cannot infer safety or effectiveness from payload fit alone. Human incident command approves the protected area; the aircraft authority retains flight, loading, release, diversion, and abort authority.

## Consequences

### Positive
- Preserves the high-value concept while preventing speculative dimensions from becoming operational claims.
### Negative
- Aerodynamics, materials, aircraft integration, robotics, fire science, and range testing create a long research program.
### Neutral
- Blanket deployment supplements containment; it does not make vegetation fireproof or replace durable firelines.
