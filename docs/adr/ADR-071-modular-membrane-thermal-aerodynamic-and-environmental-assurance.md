# ADR-071: Modular membrane thermal, aerodynamic, and environmental assurance

- **Status**: proposed
- **Date**: 2026-07-21
- **Deciders**:
- **Tags**: materials, membrane, safety

## Context

A lightweight membrane large enough to cover strategic ground can generate severe extraction/opening/tension/sail loads and can tear, loft, trap heat/smoke, damage vegetation/infrastructure, interfere with aircraft/responders, or create contaminated waste. Preliminary area density and thermal claims are research targets.

## Decision

Use independently isolatable, replaceable, uniquely identified panels with rip-stop boundaries, controlled vents/reefing, reinforced nodes, sacrificial overload connectors, bounded tethers/reels, anchors, temperature/ember/tension/contact sensing, and known failure modes. Maintain a configuration-controlled material and joint qualification dossier covering areal density/variability, packed volume, tensile/tear/puncture/abrasion/fold fatigue, permeability/vent behavior, radiant/convective/flame/ember exposure, heat transfer below, smoke/toxic products, water/retardant compatibility, conductivity/static/lightning, UV/moisture/freeze-thaw, contamination/cleaning, reuse, recovery and disposal. Couple structural/aeroelastic/fire/terrain models with coupon, panel, multi-panel and full-system tests. A panel may isolate or release only toward a precomputed safe state/zone; sacrificial release is an irreversible audited event. Claims use measured duration/flux/conditions, never “fireproof.”

## Consequences

### Positive
- Contains local failures and makes material effectiveness and environmental cost measurable.
### Negative
- Mass, strength, thermal protection, venting, packability and recoverability impose competing constraints.
### Neutral
- Final dimensions, 0.35 kg/m² target, and 100-panel count remain configuration hypotheses until qualified.
